# Brand Package Prompt for Janet "Dara" Jeffus

## Personal Brand Identity

**Name:** Janet Jeffus  
**Nickname/Handle:** Dara  
**Title:** Technologist, Hacker & Software Engineer  
**Experience:** 20+ years in software engineering and leadership  
**LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/janet-jeffus-b0709720/

## Brand Essence

A sophisticated blend of professional elegance and retro-tech nostalgia. The brand should communicate:
- **Professional credibility** as a seasoned CTO and engineer
- **Technical authenticity** rooted in hacker culture (Wozniak/Torvalds lineage)
- **Aesthetic harmony** between cyberpunk, chiptune, and classic computing eras
- **Organized sophistication** with a terminal/command-line aesthetic
- **Technical leadership** with a focus on feminine yet cutting-edge
  feel

## Visual Identity Requirements

### Color Palette
Create a cohesive color scheme that includes:
- **Primary accent colors:** Neon/bright colors reminiscent of:
  - Terminal green (#00FF00 or similar)
  - Cyberpunk cyan/magenta (#00FFFF, #FF00FF)
  - Classic amber terminal (#FFB000)
- **Secondary colors:** 
  - Deep dark backgrounds (#0D0D0D to #1A1A1A)
  - Mid-tone grays for text (#CCCCCC, #888888)
- **Accent highlights:**
  - 8-bit/16-bit game palette colors
  - Retro computer beige/cream (#F5F5DC)
  - CRT screen glow effects

### Typography
Select fonts that balance:
- **Monospace/Terminal fonts** for code and technical content:
  - Examples: JetBrains Mono, Fira Code, IBM Plex Mono, Source Code Pro, Courier Prime
- **Clean sans-serif** for headers and professional content:
  - Modern geometric sans-serifs that don't compete with the retro aesthetic
  - Should feel organized and elegant
- **Pixel fonts** (optional, for decorative elements):
  - 8-bit/16-bit style fonts for nostalgic touches

### Logo & Visual Marks
Design elements should include:
- A **primary logo/wordmark** that can work in both:
  - Terminal/ASCII art style
  - Clean vector format for professional contexts
- **Icon/avatar** considerations:
  - Could incorporate pixel art aesthetic (reference: uploaded pixel art avatar)
  - Terminal cursor or command prompt elements
  - Geometric/technical patterns
- **ASCII art signatures** or decorative elements
- **Terminal emulator frame** as a design motif

### Visual Style Elements
- **Scan lines** and CRT effects (subtle)
- **Grid patterns** suggesting circuit boards or terminal windows
- **Glitch aesthetics** (minimal, tasteful)
- **Geometric shapes** with technical precision
- **Pixel art elements** integrated with modern design
- **Terminal window frames** as content containers
- **Blinking cursor** animations

## Brand Personality

### Voice & Tone
- **Professional but approachable:** Expert without being intimidating
- **Technically authentic:** Uses proper terminology, honors hacker culture
- **Confident and direct:** No unnecessary fluff
- **Subtly playful:** Easter eggs and nods to computing history

### Cultural References
Draw inspiration from:
- **Classic computing:** Commodore 64, Apple II, early Unix terminals
- **Hacker culture:** MIT AI Lab, early open source, homebrew computer club
- **Cyberpunk aesthetics:** Neon, urban tech, digital underground
- **Chiptune music:** 8-bit sound, tracker software, demoscene
- **Terminal/CLI tools:** vim, emacs, tmux, ASCII art

## Technical Authenticity Markers

Include references to:
- **Terminal commands** and shell prompts
- **Classic programming languages:** C, Perl, Lisp, Assembly
- **Unix philosophy:** Do one thing well, pipes and composability
- **Version control:** Git, open source collaboration
- **Hardware hacking:** Circuit boards, soldering, maker culture
- **Retro computers:** Actual model names and specifications

## Brand Positioning Statement

"Janet 'Dara' Jeffus is a veteran software engineer and CEO who bridges the gap between old-school hacker ethos and modern technical leadership. Her brand celebrates the elegance of command-line interfaces, the creativity of early computing, and the precision of well-crafted code—all while maintaining the professional credibility of a 20-year veteran in tech leadership."

## Competition/Inspiration

Reference style similar to:
- **Liz The Developer** (lizthe.dev) - professional but personality-driven
- **Terminal/CLI portfolio sites** - functional ASCII interfaces
- **Demoscene websites** - technical + artistic
- **Classic computer museums** - nostalgic but organized

## Must Avoid

- ❌ Generic "tech blue" corporate aesthetics
- ❌ Overly feminine or cutesy design (unless intentionally subversive)
- ❌ Modern minimalism that feels cold or sterile
- ❌ Designs that look amateurish or "hobbyist"
- ❌ Pure kitsch without substance
- ❌ Anything that undermines professional credibility

## Success Criteria

The brand should make viewers think:
- ✅ "This person really knows their stuff"
- ✅ "They've been in tech since the golden age"
- ✅ "Technical expertise meets creative vision"
- ✅ "Professional but has personality"
- ✅ "I want to work with this person"
